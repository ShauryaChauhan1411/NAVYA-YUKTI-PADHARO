The Artisan's Legacy: Treasures to Carry Home

Rajasthan's handicrafts are a direct reflection of its rich history, offering authentic souvenirs for every traveler. These items are crafted using skills passed down through centuries, ensuring you take home a piece of the state's living heritage.

Key Handicrafts

Jewellery & Gemstones

Famous for exquisite gold and silver jewellery featuring Kundan (setting gemstones with gold foil) and Meenakari (colourful enamel work). Jaipur is one of the world's largest centres for cutting and polishing precious stones.

Mojris (Juttis)

Traditional, handcrafted leather footwear often embroidered with complex threadwork, beads, or mirrors. These backless, colourful shoes are comfortable and stylish additions to any wardrobe.

Blue Pottery

Distinctive, turquoise-blue glazed pottery (though it is a mix of green, blue, and yellow). The art is quartz-based (not clay), making it fragile but unique. Items include decorative plates, vases, and utility boxes.

Textiles & Prints

Look for Bandhani (tie-dye) scarves, sarees, and turbans, famed for their intricate patterns and vibrant colours. Also popular are the intricate block-printed fabrics of Sanganeri and Bagru.
